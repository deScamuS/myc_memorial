Crew Prize

The Prize

The A model itself is of Brad Robinson's Melges A-scow "Victory"...


It will be presented to the top crew in any fleet or event that is held at Minnetonka Yacht Club in the year it is awarded. A crew member could also be a helper at a social event, like a regatta or other significant event hosted by The Minnetonka Yacht Club.

Fleets and fleet captains will be advised to recommend people who should be considered for this award. A recommendation along with why they should win the award will be submitted to MYC for an eventual winner. It will be awarded annually at the Minnetonka Yacht Club Banquet.

The model is of the first A, the 1986 Melges, not the 2003 hull. They look the same from a galloping horse, but there are clear differences to those of us who sailed on or against both of them, notably the vang configuration, the boomkin, and the rudders.

The model shows the 1986 hull in her 1986 livery, when she was configured with symmetrical(conventional) spinnakers; she was later modified in 1996 with a bowsprit, to carry asymmetrical spinnakers. The 1986 hull was also the first in the national fleet to convert to foil shaped, high aspect ratio rudders. That modification had several iterations (they kept breaking off), and took place about 1990. The model was made by Kyle Metzloff, of Madison, Wisconsin. Unlike the Stu Lemon models (hand carved), the Metzloff crafting was done with a CNC milling machine, with excruciatingly correct detail.

The DeSantis Children (Rachel, Michael, Elizabeth, Jason & Anna) are dedicating it to the memory of parents Gary & Cheryl DeSantis and Brad Robinson.
Thank you all who have helped us in this life - you will be loved forever... and thank you Minnetonka Yacht Club for hosting this prize and keeping alive the memories of the ones who had an impact on us and this lake we called home. Peace

Gary & Cheryl DeSantis

Let's start with a story...

On one particularly windy day, the normal races were cancelled due to high winds. We decided that we would race the two boats around Big Island...we were adventurous. Gary was on the A boat with 7 people, and Cheryl was on my E boat with 5 people. This is a very large crew for the E boat, never ever had this many people on board. Fun fact, she was pregnant with Rachel, which will tell you how long ago this happened. A huge puff came out of the Northwest as we were sailing up the bay towards Big Island. Wind was pretty much out of the Crystal Bay neighborhood where Sailors World was located. We watched as it hit the A boat ahead of us and laughed as they almost flipped it. Then it hit us. We went over, and capsized very quickly. When I went under the boat in the water to check the lines, my main sheet was still cleated hard in the Harken block...it was my fault that we rolled. Tom Traff was watching over us and came immediately to our/Cheryl's rescue. It was like he didn't even acknowledge the rest of us in the water. Too funny. We all knew that Cheryl was pregnant, and Tom was shouting "Cheryl! Cheryl! - Can we bring you on board". We all survived, although my ego was a little bruised. Cheryl made it, and so did Rachel, so technically we had 6 people on board the E boat that day.
-Danny Crear

Why

Gary and Charyl DeSantis - owners/operators of Sailors World Marina and Boat Club on Smiths Bay, where many members filled their gas tanks and purchased sailboats, had become well known and loved throughout the community... and with 5 lunatic kids in tow they strived to keep Sailors World a place for all to feel welcome and important.
Gary was a long standing crew as a main sail/spinnaker trimmer on Willie Crear's many Ascows with some pretty cool stories - like when my dad climbed the Ascow mast to the spreaders in the middle of a race and peed on Willie and the crew :) Gary and wife Cheryl also crewed on Dan Crears' "E" boat and raced at Wayzata Club quite a bit. Cheryl was also a crew, and was best know for her willingness to help out with social events and parties throughout her life. Always the first to jump in and help.

Growing up in Minnetonka Beach

Growing up with his mother, father and sister (Eleverna, Ernie & Mary DeSantis) on Mtka Beach down the shore from Layfyette Club, he soon found Cheryl Sayler, who together, would make Sailors World on Smiths Bay a home away from home for many throughout the community.




Brad Robinson

Brad Robinson sailed his last race on October 17, 2015, leaving behind an enduring legacy of honor, passion and generosity to the sailing community he loved.


In 1949 Robinson enrolled at Minnetonka Sailing School, where he was involved for the rest of his life as a student, instructor, chairman and board member, growing alongside it. His Scow sailing career spanned nearly seven decades.

Brad Robinson was a scow sailor... Growing up on the shores of Lake Minnetonka, he discovered his passion at a young age and spent much of his competitive sailing career developing ways to improve the performance of his sailboats. In retirement, this drive for advancement in sailboat design and technology led him to establish Victory By Design, LLC. He then built Victory, a class legal A Scow, to be the proof boat for his real dream of a totally carbon fiber ultra light V38: InnoVation.
